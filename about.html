Philosophy & mission

Beyond
machines

Langen exists because of a simple conviction: that a motorcycle should be extraordinary to ride and extraordinary to look at. That engineering precision and emotional connection are not in opposition — they are the same thing. Every decision we make, from material selection to throttle calibration, serves that belief.

Our mission
To build motorcycles that move the soul. Machines where exceptional technical performance and the intangible purity of the riding experience are inseparable — where every component earns its place and nothing exists without purpose.
Our philosophy
Simplify, then add lightness. A natural, unfiltered connection between rider and machine on every level — mechanical, emotional, visceral. No unnecessary electronics between you and the road. No compromise dressed up as a feature.
Our approach
Low-volume, high-detail manufacturing with the highest UK local content of any motorcycle manufacturer. Every Langen is a bespoke creation, built to measure for the individual who ordered it. We do not operate production lines — we operate a workshop, and we know every motorcycle by name.

Our story

The Langen
journey

Langen Motorcycles began in 2018 when founder Christofer Ratcliffe — then Chief Design Engineer at CCM — set out to build the motorcycle he had always wanted to ride. Not a concept. Not a render. A real, road-legal machine that would be the lightest, most involving thing on two wheels.

In 2019, a partnership with Vincenzo Mattia and Vins Motors in Maranello brought the engine to life: a 249.5cc two-stroke V-twin unlike anything the road had seen in two decades. The Two Stroke launched at Salon Privé, Blenheim Palace, to immediate critical acclaim. All 100 builds were allocated. Langen had arrived.

"We do things differently here."

The team that made it happen was drawn from the best of British automotive and motorsport engineering — CCM, Triumph, BAC, McLaren and M-Sport. Not recruited for CVs, but for a shared obsession with lightweight construction, mechanical honesty and the refusal to accept "good enough."

Today, Langen is building the Lightspeed: a 185hp, 185kg motorcycle powered by a bespoke Rotax V-twin developed with Buell. It is the second chapter of the same story — and the ambition has only grown. Based in Wigan, Greater Manchester, we remain a small team with an outsized belief in what a motorcycle can be. Every component questioned. Every gram accounted for. Every rider considered.

Our process

Crafting
excellence

01
Concept & design
Every Langen begins with a clear engineering objective. Weight targets are set first, aesthetics second — and then the two are reconciled until neither compromises the other.
02
Precision engineering
Materials and components are selected with obsessive care. Carbon fibre, aerospace-grade aluminium, CNC-machined titanium — each element is chosen for its contribution to the whole, not its specification sheet.
03
Rigorous testing
Every motorcycle is subjected to performance, endurance and safety testing that goes beyond homologation requirements. Nothing leaves Wigan until it exceeds the standard we would accept for ourselves.
04
Bespoke specification
Each owner works directly with the team to specify paint, chassis finish, ergonomics, controls and suspension tuning. The result is a motorcycle built around one person — not a demographic.
05
Personal handover
Delivery is not a transaction. Every owner collects their motorcycle from the workshop, meets the team who built it, and leaves with a direct, permanent line to the people behind the machine.
